Scotland's Finest Flavours Itinerary

Threave Garden And Estate

This garden is for all seasons and is renowned for its springtime daffodil, herbaceous beds ablaze with colour in summer and dense heather gardens in autumn. The estate is a wildfowl refuge and a designated Special Protection Area, which has led to Threave’s wetlands being designated as an Area of Special Scientific Interest.

Threave Estate, Castle Douglas, Dumfries & Galloway, DG7 1RX

0844 493 2245

Tarbet

Tarbet is a small village situated on the east shores of Loch Lomond and is a popular tourist destination, attracting hill walkers, cyclists and many more. There is a wide selection of restaurants to suit all tastes, occasions and price ranges; when it comes to dining out, the choice quality and Scottish ambiance is unsurpassed.

Tarbet Hotel, Tarbet, Arrochar, Loch Lomond, Argyll & Bute, G83 7DE

01301 702346

Loch Fyne Oyster Bar

This Restaurant & Oyster Bar has had many visitors, including politicians, celebrities, film stars and royalty. The menus are drawn up on a daily basis depending on seasonal availability to ensure best use of local produce. There is also a deli here, selling both local produce and produce gained directly from Loch Fyne Oyster Bar.

Loch Fyne, Clachan, Cairndow, PA26 8BL

01499 600264

Loch Lomond Shores

This is a lochside retail complex in Balloch, offering shopping, eating, outdoor activities and events for the family. There’s a Valvona & Crolla food hall that can’t be missed as it offers the best of Scottish products, from smoked salmon to malt whisky.

Loch Lomond Shores, Ben Lomond Way, Balloch, Argyll, G83 8QL

Loch Awe

The community stretches from Bridge or Orchy in the north, to Eredine in the south and includes the longest freshwater loch in Scotland, Loch Awe. Many activities can take place here, including hill-walking, climbing, cycling and fishing. This is also a popular place for wildlife as well, with red deer, pine martens, otters and the famous Scottish wildcat being seen quite often.

The Real Food Café

Taking their sourcing very seriously, if products are not available locally, they come up with the best alternative from elsewhere to ensure that they sell the highest quality raw ingredients. This has been proved through their food winning many awards.

The Real Food Café , Tyndrum, Perthshire, Scotland, FK20 8RY

01838 400 235

Aberfeldy

Aberfeldy is a town in Highland Perthshire, situated on Scotland’s longest river, the River Tay. Outdoor adventures don’t come short in this area, with plenty of local activity providers that can take lessons in white-water rafting, abseiling, gorge walking, canyoning, mountain biking and a Highland Safari.

The House Of Bruar

Sitting like a castle at the foot of the Bruar Falls, The House of Bruar is widely acknowledged as Scotland’s most prestigious independent store. Many departments are available, including a Menswear Hall, Ladieswear and shoes and handbags. If a break from shopping is needed, then there is a Food Hall and Restaurant, full of British gourmet produce, home-cooked food and the award-winning butchery.

Blair Atholl, Pitlochry, Perthshire, PH18 5TW

Blair Castle & Gardens

Blair Castle is the ancient seat of the Dukes and Earls of Atholl and home to Europe’s last remaining private army, the Atholl Highlanders. The Tullibardine Restaurant at Blair Castle provides a range of hot or cold meals, from traditional Scottish dishes to homemade soup to cakes and traybakes. But the main attraction to the castle are the gardens, which feature a 9 acre walled garden that has been restored to its original Georgian design.

Blair Castle, Blair Atholl, Pitlochry, Perthshire, PH18 5TL

01796 481207

Moulin Inn & Brewery

The present owners had seen successful pub breweries in England and decided to build Moulin, which was one of the first microbreweries in Scotland. With the Scots seeming to like sweeter ale, the owners chose the ale that they would sell, based on what the public preferred.

Moulin Inn & Brewery, Pitlochry, Perthshire, Scotland

01796 472196

Blair Atholl

Blair Atholl is a village in Highland Perthshire with the River Garry flowing through, linking other local villages and glens, such as Calvine, Bruar, Killiecrankie and Struan. Many activities are possible in Blair Atholl, including, to name a few, bowling, curling, cycling and mountain biking, deer stalking and fishing. Whether eating in a restaurant or eating food in the park, there’s always good places to eat in Blair Atholl.

Anstruther Fish Bar & Restaurant

This is a multi-award-winning Bar and Restaurant, voted Best in Scotland 2000/01 and 2001/02. The owners personally source only the finest quality seafood every morning through their sister company Argofish. It has an extensive seafood menu, including Scottish haddock, halibut, hake and lemon sole through to local crab and lobster, which is all sourced sustainably.

42-44 Shore St, Anstruther, KY10 3AQ

01333 310518

St Andrews Farmhouse Cheese Company

The business started because nobody else in Fife was making cheese from their own milk on the farm and there seemed to be demand for and interest in locally produced food. In January 2008, their first cheese was made and production has continued ever since. Their cheese is available to buy from the Coffee Shop, along with a selection of other British Cheeses, all made by farmers like themselves, using their home-produced milk to make an artisan cheese on the farm.

Falside Farm, Anstruther, KY10 2RT

01333 312580
