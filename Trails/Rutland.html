The Olive Branch

In the kitchen they create wonderful pub classics alongside some more 'restaurant style' dishes all using local, seasonal and fresh ingredients. Alongside the daily changing menu there is also is a daily changes set lunch, salads and sandwiches from a blackboard menu in the summer and soups and sandwiches in the winter. In addition to great food they serve locally brewed real ales, farmhouse bottled ciders and continental & British craft lagers. For the summer they make their own speciality country cocktails, pimms and strawberry vodka and mulled wine and sloe gin in the winter.

Main Street, Clipsham, Rutland, LE15 7SH

Greetham Valley Hotel, Golf and Conference Centre

The delicious seasonal menus offer a wide range of choice and their restaurants enjoy panoramic views over the golf courses, lakes and valley. Everyone is welcome to dine at Greetham Valley – you don’t need to be golfers, Club members or a Hotel guest! Head chef Andy Winter and his excellent team take the greatest care in selecting the best and freshest of local ingredients to prepare meals. Many of the ingredients are grown including sweet, juicy strawberries when in season, a wide variety of home grown herbs, succulent salmon and trout are caught in two of the lakes. The farm in the village supplies free range eggs, whilst asparagus travels just a few miles from nearby Rutland Water as does trout. Bread is baked in the local market town and all fruit and vegetables are sourced from the nearest counties. Ray Camplejohn from Leicestershire has been supplying our meat for 19 years and sources our melt in the mouth steaks specially for us.

Wood Lane, Greetham, Oakham, Rutland, LE15 7SN

01780 460444

The Kings Arms Inn

Award winning cuisine and a wonderful British country pub with a community feel. They serve traditional, artisan and innovative pub fare on rustic wooden tables, surrounded by beamed ceilings, flagstone floors and open fires. The bar is brimming with a large selection of homemade snacks including salami’s, biltong, pork scratchings and smoked cashew nuts. Bottles of homemade sloe gin, Elderflower vodka and Zermatter mulled wine are all made with berries from the surrounding hedgerows, and fruit from neighbours. A selection of well kept local real ales, regional and local ciders, fruit presse’s and traditional soft drinks are always available. Speciality wines at affordable prices

13 Top Street, Wing, Rutland, LE15 8SE

Barnsdale Lodge Hotel

The kitchen brigade use locally sourced, seasonal produce. Local Suppliers include Manton Farm, for asparagus, raspberries and strawberries and Grasmere Farm for pork. They source their game from Easton Estate, approximately 12 miles from the hotel. They have a kitchen garden in the hotel grounds, growing vegetables and herbs which are used on many of the dishes in the restaurant. The rare-breed hens also provide beautiful, fresh eggs. Barnsdale Lodge's restaurant is open to residents and non-residents for breakfast, morning coffee, lunch, afternoon tea and dinner and is renowned for its private dining.
