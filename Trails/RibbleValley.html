Ferguson's Deli

This is the place to come for the best broth in Lancashire, according to many. Using recipes that have been passed down to him, the proprietor bakes, cooks and prepares all his products on-site, using as much local meat, poultry and vegetables as possible from local producers.

Exchange Coffee

Exchange Coffee stocks over 30 varieties of coffee and just over 50 loose teas, with the belief that the secret to good coffee is freshness. They buy ‘green’ coffee from across the world to be roasted in small batches. As well as coffee, they also sell meat, fish, jam and make their own cakes.

Exchange Coffee Company, 24 Wellgate, Clitheroe, Lancashire, BB7 3DP

01200 442270

Wellgate Fisheries

The most popular fish merchants in the North West, providing fish and seafood to the region’s most popular restaurants, as well as selling seafood and fish in his Clitheroe shop and stall at Clitheroe market. Their philosophy is to buy the best fish available, by supporting Manchester wholesale fish market and buying direct from the Lancashire and Cumbrian coasts.

Wellgate Fisheries, 5 Wellgate, Clitheroe, Lancashire, BB7 2DS

01200 423511

The Gibbon Bridge Hotel

Janet, the creator of The Gibbon Bridge Hotel, is a champion of local suppliers, including Leagram’s Organic Dairy for cheese, Johnson and Swarbrick for poultry, Brendan Anderton for meat, Bowland Brewery for beer and Ann Forshaw’s Alston Dairy for yoghurt. Leagram Organic Dairy

This dairy has multi award-winning handmade and waxed organic cheeses direct from his Soil Association-accredited dairy, or many local outlets. Organic sheet milk is used, while cows milk comes from suppliers within a 10 mile radius of the dairy. Organic, creamy, crumbly, mature, soft Lancashire cheese is made on-site, while cheeses are also available with different flavours, including, ginger, apricot and garlic.

Leagram Organic Dairy, High Head Farm Buildings, Green Lane, Chipping, PR3 2TQ

01995 61532

Little Town Dairy

This Dairy specialises in premium yoghurt and crème fraiche made with the milk of their RSPCA-accredited herd. Dairy produce includes crème fraiche, smoothies, luxury thick and creamy yogurts, with real fruit, and farmhouse ice cream. Beef in the farm shop is family-farm Aberdeen Angus, which is hung for up to three weeks, while pork is from their own and locally-sourced and lamb from Little Town or local farms. Bread, cakes and pies are also sold, as well as local jams, preserves, honey, pickles, chutneys and seasonal vegetables.

Little Town Dairy, Farm Shop and Tea Room, Chipping Road, Thornley, Lancashire, PR3 2TB
