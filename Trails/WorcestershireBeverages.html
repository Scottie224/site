Beverages

Astley Vineyards

This English vineyard was established in 1971 and has been producing international award-winning white wines with PDO status (Protect Designation of Origin). Walks can be taken around the vineyard, followed by wine tasting sessions in the Cellar Door shop.

Astley, Stourport-on-Severn, Worcestershire, DY13 0RU

01299 822907

Barbourne Cider & Perry

This family-run business hand pick and press Worcestershire cider apples and perry pears from Worcestershire orchards, to make a range of traditional ciders and perries, which are available in bottles or as draught.

19 York Place, Worcester, WR1 3DR

01905 27151

Nurses Cottage Drinks

This is a traditional cottage producer of cordials and presses, including the award-wining Mulled Plum Potion and the sparkling Elderflower Pressé as well as the classics like the Elderflower Pressé and the original Plum Pressé drinks.

Rectory Cottage, Wick Road, Little Comberton, Pershore, WR10 3EG

01386 710447

Pershore College

This is a national centre of excellence for horticulture, who set up the apple juice and cider enterprise in 1993 to add value to the fruit grown in their orchards. They produce over 40,000 bottles of their own brand apple juice and 7,000 litres of cider. Both of which have won many regional and national competitions. They also produce organic juices, perry and lemonade.

Avonbank, Pershore, Worcestershire, WR10 3JP

01386 551151

Rose Bank Vineyard

After starting the business in 2003, the range of wines available have won National awards. This reflects the quality of the grapes grown and the wine making by Three Choirs Vineyards Ltd in Newent, Gloucestershire.

247 Droitwich Road, Fernhill Heath, Nr. Worcester, WR3 7UH

01905 451439

Tardebigge Cider

Tardebigge Cider is an artisan or craft cider producer, having been registered with the customs and excise in 2000. They have received many prestigious awards for the quality of their cider, with the main reason being that all ingredients are locally sourced. They have also started producing Perry, Cider Vinegar and occasionally apple juice.

Tutnall, Bromsgrove, B60 1NB

01527 877946 / 07712 223371

The Fleece Inn

With real ales aplenty and makers of The Ark Cider, this historic pub is a favourite with CAMRA members and the welcoming atmosphere and good food attracts visitors from all over the world.

The Cross, Bretforton, Evesham, WR11 7JE

01386 831173

The Holywell Water Company Ltd.

This is the original source of bottled Malvern spring water, which shows that the production began in 1622. The refreshing taste of the water tastes the same as it did in the 17th century, with the current custodians ensuring its heritage is protected for future generations to enjoy.

The Holywell, Holywell Road, Malvern Wells, WR14 4LE

01684 568548

Tiltridge Vineyard

This small, mature vineyard produces sparkling, still white and rose wines, which consistently win awards at national level. Their taste is crisp clean and fruity, typical of the English style which has become popular over the last few years.

Upper Hook Road, Upton-upon-Severn, WR8 0SA

01684 592906
