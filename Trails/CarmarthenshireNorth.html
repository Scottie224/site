Carmarthenshire North Food Trail

Caws Cenarth

This quarter of a century old family business is producing award-winning cheeses, as well as Prince Charles’ favourite cheese, Perl Las, a strong blue cheese with a mellow creamy after taste.

Glyneithinog Farm, Lancych, Boncath, SA37 0LH

01239 710432

Cothi Valley Produce

This specialises in the goat and cheese-making facilities. Producing a range of goat milk cheeses, with the aim of emphasising the owners’ Kentish roots and others inspired by Carmarthenshire.

Cilwr Farm, Talley, Llandeilo, Carmarthenshire, SA19 7BQ

01558 685555

Jacobi Brewery Caio

Justin Jacobi, the owner, manager and brewer of this microbrewery, turns out 1,200 bottles a week, including a seasonal range and four permanent brews, some of which are award-winning.

Penlanwen Farm, Pumsaint, SA19 8RR

01558 650605

Harrisons Bistro & Riverside Garden

The place to go for a big breakfast, hearty lunches, afternoon teas and an evening menu, all with views over the castle and river.

Sycamore St, Newcastle, Emlyn

01239 711814

The Black Lion at Abergorlech

A traditional village pub serving great, local food, where the beer garden, found next to the river Gorlech, has been voted as one of the best in Britain by readers of the Times. Welcomes muddy mountain bikers and walkers.

Abergorlech, Carmarthenshire, SA32 7SN

01558 685271

The Carrot Cruncher

The place to go for all environmentally conscious shoppers, selling organic foods, fresh fruit and vegetables.

Newcastle, Emlyn, Market Hall

01239 710732

The Drunken Sailor

Selling local seafood, including fresh, local crabs, fish and shellfish and homemade dishes, such as seafood chowder and many more.

1 Market Sq, Newcastle, Emlyn

07831 251923

Tregroes Waffles

Run entirely by locals, in the town of Llandysul in North Carmarthenshire are the best waffles, made with a butter toffee filling; a treat that goes well with tea or coffee or ice cream.

Tregroes Waffles, Unit 2, Pencader Road, Llandysul, SA44 4AE

01559 363468

Ty Te Twn

Set in the countryside, this is the place to go for a cup of tea and a homemade Welshcake, looking over Rhandirmwyn village.

Twm’s Tea House, Rhandirmwyn

01239 711356
