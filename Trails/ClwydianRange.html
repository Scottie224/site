The Clwydian Range Food Trail

Caffi Florence

Fresh, local, seasonal, homemade food. A taste of wales with great food, all homemade from scratch using the best locally source, seasonal ingredients. Day menu includes breakfast and brunch section, homemade soups, main and light lunches, desserts, traditional English and Welsh afternoon teas and a great range of cakes and quality teas and fairtrade coffee. They specialise in making the best afternoon tea by using leaves in real tea pots to make a seriously good cup of tea. Featured on BBC’s Rip Off Britain with great review and compliments.

Loggerheads Country Park, Ruthin Road, Loggerheads, Denbighshire, CH7 5LH

01352 810397

Rhesgoad Farm Shop

A family run business located on a working farm in an area of natural beauty. They produce the finest Welsh Lamb and Pork in the Clwydian Range. They offer locally produced products of poultry and beef along with homemade sausages from their home reared pork.

Rhesgoed Farm, Llanbedr Dyffryn Clwyd, Ruthin, LL151YE

01824703090

WG Jones

WG Jones offers beautiful cuts of meat and they pride themselves in quality bacon and sausages and have won True Taste Awards. They also feature great cotemporary classics and hot dishes from the extensive WG Jones menu.

Well Street, Ruthin, LL151AF

01824 702807

Leonardo's Delicatessen

Purveyors of fine food & wines, specialising in bespoke takeaways and outside catering. Leonardo’s Delicatessen has won several national awards including not only, Champion Pie (with their Chicken, Leek & Laver bread pie) at the British Pie awards in 2011, but also a Gold and Bronze award for their Steak & Kidney Pie and Chicken, Chilli & Cheese Pasty. They also achieved a Silver Award for the delicatessen at the True Taste of Wales 2011. They offer a wide range of local and continental cheeses, homemade pies, quiches, salads, pâtés, olives and charcuterie, as well as a wide range of Welsh wines, spirits and liqueurs. They also stock Charbonnel et Walker and Booja Booja chocolates alongside chocolates and fudges produced in Wales.

4 Well Street, Ruthin, LL151AE

01824707161

Eagle’s Bakery

Located in the market town of Ruthin, is Eagle’s Bakery. The owner and master baker, Wyn Roberts rises at 3am every morning to ensure that the bread is prepared and baked ready to be sold for the day.

28 Clwyd Street, Ruthin, LL15 1HH

Williams Butchers

Opened in 2012, this butchers has since then won the North and South Wales award for excellence in meat products, Silver and Gold awards for lamb and mince pies and their famous chicken tikka pie. All of the animals are reared locally and produced on the premises, so the customers know they are getting great quality and value for money. They produce their own homemade sausages, burgers, black pudding, dry cured bacon and pies.

124 Vale Street, Denbigh, LL16 3BS

Old Forge Trout Farm

A family-run business that has consistently produced excellent quality trout for over 25 years. They supply to wholesale markets, local public houses and restaurants and trout fisheries along the North Wales coast. They also have a stocked farm shop selling a wide variety of game, meats, pickles, chutneys, market fresh sea fish and their own freshly caught trout and smoked produce.

Llandyrnog Creamery

Supplying all major UK food retailers, many of its largest food processors and food service organisations, Milk Link manufacturers a wide range of dairy products including, long life milk, extended shelf life products, flavoured milks and milk powders. Milk Link if also the largest producer of cheese in the UK, making a large range of award winning British cheeses.

Clyttir Farm Shop

David Sharples is passionate about the quality of the eggs produced at his farm and consequently are fully compliant with the national Welfare of Laying Hens Directive. The business has been built around the principal of localness, with the fresh eggs being delivered to local businesses and sold to local residents throughout the day.

Clyttir Farm, Llanbedr Dyffryn Clwyd, Ruthin, LL15 1TU

01824 702332

Rosies Triple D Cider

Becoming a full-time cidermaker in 2011, this cider is farm pressed, fermented and matured at Rosies Triple D Cider. After achieving further competition success, to support sales at the farmers markets and food festivals, the cider shop was opened at the local farm.
